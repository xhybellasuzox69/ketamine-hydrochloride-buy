## What is Ketamine Hydrochloride?
Ketamine hydrochloride is a dissociative anesthetic medication primarily used in medical and veterinary settings. Discovered in the 1960s, it has gained renewed attention in 2026 for its potential in treating depression and chronic pain. Unlike traditional anesthetics, ketamine works by blocking NMDA receptors in the brain, inducing a trance-like state while maintaining breathing and airway reflexes.

In medical contexts, it's administered via injection, nasal spray, or infusion. The FDA approved esketamine (a form of ketamine) for treatment-resistant depression in 2019, and by 2026, ongoing research highlights its role in mental health therapies.

### Medical and Therapeutic Applications
- **Anesthesia**: Widely used in emergency surgery and pediatric care due to its safety profile.
- **Pain Management**: Effective for neuropathic pain and complex regional pain syndrome.
- **Mental Health**: Low-dose ketamine infusions show promise for PTSD, anxiety, and suicidal ideation, with clinics expanding access globally.
- **Veterinary Use**: Common in animal sedation, available through licensed suppliers.

Research in 2026 emphasizes ketamine's rapid antidepressant effects, often working within hours compared to weeks for SSRIs.

## Risks and Safety Considerations
While beneficial, ketamine carries risks including dissociation, elevated blood pressure, and potential for abuse. Long-term use may lead to bladder issues (ketamine cystitis). In 2026, guidelines from the WHO stress monitored administration to minimize harms.

**Checklist for Safe Use**:
- Verify prescription authenticity.
- Use only in clinical settings.
- Monitor for side effects like hallucinations or nausea.
- Avoid combining with alcohol or opioids.
- Report adverse reactions to healthcare providers.
